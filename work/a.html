Role charter / executive mandate

The Head of AI and Data Transformation serves as the enterprise leader accountable for shaping Allianz’s AI and data agenda across global operating entities, aligning business priorities with Allianz Technology execution, and ensuring transformation delivers measurable value with strong governance, responsible AI controls, and scalable operating discipline.

01

Enterprise AI & Data Strategy

Mission and leadership mandate
Set one enterprise vision for AI and data transformation that aligns Group priorities, OE needs, and Allianz Technology delivery capabilities.
  • Define enterprise AI and data strategy and target outcomes
  • Translate strategy into business value across Life, P&C, and Health
  • Act as executive advisor to business and technology leadership
  • Lead buy / build / partner choices for strategic capabilities
02

Allianz Group, AzT , OE's Alignment

Federated enterprise Ops model
Create a support model that balances enterprise standards and reusable platforms with local OE flexibility across markets, regulations, and maturity levels.
  • Establish central guardrails with federated OE execution
  • Stand up OE enablement waves based on value and readiness
  • Create reusable playbooks, reference architectures, and capability templates
  • Coordinate regionally while preserving country-specific regulatory needs
03

Transformation & Modernization

Business and technology change
Accelerate transformation across underwriting, claims, policy administration, customer operations, and data platform evolution using an outcome-led model.
  • Modernize data architecture, data products, and access patterns
  • Enable MLOps / GenAIOps and scalable AI product delivery
  • Drive policy administration and claims process modernization
  • Embed Lean Portfolio Management and value-stream-based prioritization
04

Governance, Value & Responsible AI

Control, compliance, and outcomes
Govern transformation through data quality, privacy, AI controls, value realization, benefits management, and executive transparency.
  • Stand up AI governance council and data governance council
  • Embed model lifecycle, privacy, fairness, and human oversight controls
  • Track benefits through KPI dashboards, flow metrics, and executive reporting
  • Apply FinOps and funding discipline across platforms, data, and AI services

90-day, 180-day, and 12-month roadmap

A pragmatic transformation sequence that starts with mandate clarity and governance, moves into prioritized pilots and foundational platform decisions, and then scales through OE adoption, reusable enterprise capabilities, and measurable value realization.

First 90 Days

Mobilize

Confirm executive mandate, complete OE and capability maturity assessment, establish AI/data governance, define target operating model principles, shortlist top enterprise use cases, and align a joint execution cadence with Allianz Technology Near shore/offshore.

Key actions: stakeholder map, current-state diagnostic, value-stream heatmap, use-case prioritization, AI/data policy baseline, executive forums, RACI, and roadmap v1.

KPIs: charter approved, councils launched, OE heatmap complete, priority portfolio agreed.

Mandate Diagnostic Governance

First 180 Days

Build & Pilot

Launch 3–5 priority pilots, stand up minimum viable data products and shared AI platform patterns, define reusable enterprise capabilities, and activate OE enablement waves across selected markets or functions.

Key actions: reference architecture, MLOps/GenAIOps setup, pilot delivery, change network launch, value register, architecture review flow, and funding discipline refinement.

KPIs: pilots live, shared foundation components active, adoption baselines measured.

Pilots Platform Enablement

First 12 Months

Scale

Scale successful use cases to additional OEs, embed governance into BAU, strengthen portfolio steering, mature reusable AI/data services, and deliver visible business outcomes across underwriting, claims, service, pricing, and customer operations.

Key actions: expand OE coverage, institutionalize councils, role-based training by country/function, optimize cloud and AI spend, publish year-two roadmap.

KPIs: scaled use cases, throughput gains, cycle time improvement, adoption rates, documented benefits.

Scale Adoption Value realization

Risk & Mitigation Focus

Control

Key risks include fragmented OE priorities, over-centralization, duplicated local solutions, weak data quality, uncontrolled GenAI experimentation, unclear decision rights, and low adoption of new tools and workflows.

Mitigation actions: federated model, common standards, gated governance, reusable components, role-based change management, KPI transparency, and value-based funding decisions.

Transformation Delivery Model & OE Enablement

Delivery Specific

Create a delivery model that connects central transformation leadership, Allianz Technology, and operating entities through a common execution rhythm. Evolve from project-centric delivery to a transformation / value management model using Lean Portfolio Management, OE enablement waves, product-centric teams, and cross-functional governance that supports both shared capabilities and local market execution.

Focus: TMO / VMO structure, delivery cadence, prioritization, dependency management, and OE rollout playbooks.

Outcome: improved execution speed, stronger alignment, and scalable transformation adoption across regions.

Data Platform, MLOps & GenAIOps Foundations

Data + AI

Build the foundational platform capabilities required to industrialize AI and data delivery across Allianz. This includes enterprise data pipelines, governed access patterns, model development environments, model registry, CI/CD, monitoring, retraining workflows, prompt/application controls, and secure deployment patterns in close partnership with Allianz Technology.

Focus: platform standardization, reusable services, automation, observability, and deployment discipline.

Outcome: faster pilot-to-production cycles, lower duplication, stronger resilience, and scalable AI operations.

Data Governance & Data Product Model

Data Specific

Define a federated data governance model that treats data as a strategic asset and organizes critical domains into reusable data products across customer, policy, claims, underwriting, distribution, finance, and risk. Improve trust and reuse by embedding stewardship, lineage, data quality, ownership, access controls, and common business semantics across enterprise and OE-specific environments.

Focus: data ownership, quality rules, metadata, lineage, stewardship, and domain accountability.

Outcome: trusted, reusable, and scalable data foundations for AI, reporting, and operational decision-making.

Data Governance Data Products Data Quality

Responsible AI & Model Governance

AI Specific

Stand up a practical responsible AI framework covering model governance, fairness, transparency, human oversight, explainability, auditability, and lifecycle controls. Ensure AI and GenAI solutions used across Allianz operating entities are governed through clear approval gates, model inventories, monitoring standards, and risk-based policies aligned to insurance and data privacy expectations.

Focus: AI governance council, model risk management, documentation, controls, and oversight.

Outcome: trusted AI adoption at scale with lower regulatory, reputational, and operational risk.

Responsible AI Model Risk Governance

AI Strategy & Use Case Factory

AI Specific

Establish an Allianz-wide AI strategy and use case factory to identify, assess, and prioritize high-value opportunities across underwriting, claims, pricing, service, fraud, actuarial, and distribution. Create a repeatable intake-to-scale model that evaluates business value, feasibility, regulatory risk, data readiness, and reuse potential across operating entities.

Focus: enterprise AI agenda, use case triage, prioritization model, pilot-to-scale discipline.

Outcome: a governed pipeline of AI opportunities tied to value streams and business outcomes.
